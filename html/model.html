Linear predictor

The time-to-recurrence and melanoma specific mortality was estimated using a cox proportional hazards model. The linear predictor of the model is given by: $$ \begin{align*} LP &=2.031 \\ &+1.026 \cdot \text{SNstatus} \\ &+0.351 \cdot \log(\text{Age at SN})\\ &+0.491 \cdot \text{Ulceration}\\ &+0.262 \cdot \text{location_leg}+0.380 \cdot \text{location_trunk}+0.706 \cdot \text{location_hn}\\ &+0.821 \cdot (\log(\text{Breslow thickness}) - 0.715)\\ &+0.187 \cdot \log(\text{tumor burden}) \\ &-0.384 \cdot (\log(\text{Breslow thickness}) - 0.715) \cdot \text{SNstatus} \end{align*} $$ where log is the natural logarithm, SNstatus is 1 if positive SN status and 0 if negative SN status; ulceration is 1 if there is ulceration; location_leg is 1 if the location is leg; location_trunk is 1 if the location is trunk, location_hn is 1 if location is head and neck.

Recurrence

The probability of recurrence was calculated by $$ \begin{align*} \text{hazard} &= 0.253 \cdot \exp(LP) \\ P(\text{recurrence}) &= (1-\exp(-\text{hazard})) \cdot 100 \end{align*} $$ where exp the natural exponential.

Melanoma-specific mortality

The probability of melanoma-specific mortality (MSM) was calculated by $$ \begin{align*} \text{hazard} &= 0.144 \cdot \exp(1.10 \cdot LP) \\ P(\text{MSM}) &= (1-\exp(-\text{hazard})) \cdot 100 \end{align*} $$ where exp the natural exponential.
