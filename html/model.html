Linear predictor

The time-to-recurrence and melanoma specific mortality was estimated using a cox proportional hazards model. The linear predictor of the model is given by: $$ \begin{align*} LP &=- 1.283 \\ &+1.086 \cdot \text{SNstatus} \\ &+0.014 \cdot \text{Age at SN}\\ &+0.479 \cdot \text{Ulceration}\\ &+0.128 \cdot \text{location_leg} + 0.253 \cdot \text{location_trunk} + 0.636 \cdot \text{location_hn}\\ &+0.830 \cdot (\log(\text{Breslow thickness}) - 0.718)\\ &+0.212 \cdot \log(\text{tumor burden}) \\ &-0.437 \cdot (\log(\text{Breslow thickness}) - 0.718) \cdot \text{SNstatus} \end{align*} $$ where log is the natural logarithm, SNstatus is 1 if positive SN status and 0 if negative SN status; ulceration is 1 if there is ulceration; location_leg is 1 if the location is leg; location_trunk is 1 if the location is trunk, location_hn is 1 if location is head and neck.

Risk score

The risk score is calculated using $$ \text{Risk score} = (\text{LP}+4.493)/0.408 $$ Subsequently, we truncate the risk score to 17 if needed to avoid overestimation, and convert back to the linear predictor using $$ \text{LP} = \text{Risk score} \cdot 0.408-4.493 $$

Recurrence

The probability of recurrence was calculated by $$ \begin{align*} \text{hazard} &= 0.228 \cdot \exp(LP) \\ P(\text{recurrence}) &= (1-\exp(-\text{hazard})) \cdot 100 \end{align*} $$ where exp the natural exponential.

Melanoma-specific mortality

The probability of melanoma-specific mortality (MSM) was calculated by $$ \begin{align*} \text{hazard} &= 0.091 \cdot \exp(1.138 \cdot LP) \\ P(\text{MSM}) &= (1-\exp(-\text{hazard})) \cdot 100 \end{align*} $$ where exp the natural exponential.
